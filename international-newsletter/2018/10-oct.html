OCTOBER 2018, #42
Following and surrendering to a spiritual Master
Swami VishwaVijayananda, SPN
To follow and to surrender to a spiritual Master we have first to clean up our minds. We cannot take with us the baggage of the world and follow a spiritual Master. To follow a deity or a dead Master is easy, but a living Guru gives you challenges to overcome the ego which often people find difficult. To take shelter at the Guru´s feet, we have to put aside our judgmental mind. Only when the mind is anchored and focused on the feet of the Master, will we have the vision of Narayana. The Guru is there to challenge our minds. With our limited minds we cannot understand the Guru. We have made ourselves unlucky and unhappy, because we think that we need something else to be happy. Yet, the happiness is right here, inside of us.
We have forgotten that we are happy because we want to be happy. And through your happiness, everything will appear happy. Happiness doesn’t belong to anybody else. It belongs entirely to yourself. You are not happy because of somebody else.
Our happiness in life always changes. Why? Because there are always expectations, and expectations are due to the attachment we have created towards those things which we claim to be ours.
Here is a little story: There was a great yogi who was in communion with God and the yogi was blessed with great wealth. Toward the end of his life, however, the yogi developed a disease. As he lay dying, the Lord appeared to him and the great yogi said: 'Lord, I made a mistake; in my second incarnation I want to be a yogi and to know You. I care nothing for wealth, but I want to have optimum health.' In this second incarnation, the great yogi was born a yogi, knew God, and he was blessed with good health. But alas, no matter how hard he tried, he could not generate enough money to feed himself adequately. The yogi lay dying and once again the Lord came. The yogi said, 'Lord, forgive me I made another mistake. In my third incarnation I want to be a great yogi, to be in communion with You and have great health and wealth. After all, what is health without wealth to feed one’s self?'
So in the great yogi’s third incarnation, he received all those things he asked for: he was a yogi, knew God, he had great wealth and good health. However, during that lifetime the yogi became very lonely. He noticed that most others had companions and he thought that he would like a companion as well. So as he lay dying, the Lord appeared again and the great yogi said: 'Lord I made yet another mistake; what is health and wealth without the solace of a companion with whom to share life?'
So in the great yogi’s fourth incarnation, the Lord granted him health, wealth, communion with the Lord, he was a great yogi, and also gave him his dying request of a wife as companion. Alas and alack, the wife died after only two years. Once again as the yogi lay dying, the good Lord appeared. The yogi said to the Lord, 'I have made another mistake. Lord, in my fifth incarnation I want to be in communion with you, be a yogi, have wealth and health, and have a wife who lives long.'
Sure enough, in his fifth incarnation, the yogi had all his dying requests. However the wife lived too long and developed signs of old age: wrinkles, loss of beauty, etc. About that time and right on schedule, a young and lovely young girl came to work in the yogi’s household. The great yogi fell in love with the young girl’s beauty and she fell in love with his wealth. In due time, the yogi divorced his old, ugly but good wife; he then married the young, lovely girl. As one may guess, the young girl was in love with someone else. After she had obtained enough money, she left the yogi for her lover.
As the yogi lay dying, as usual, the Lord appeared. The now life-weary yogi fairly yelled: 'Lord God Almighty, I know all your tricks now. I don’t want anything but you. Wherever I am, you must be there with me; that is all I want. Always there is some problem, so now all I want is You!' And so finally the great yogi’s desires were all fulfilled by his Lord until the yogi wanted what the Lord really wanted to give him from the beginning—the Lord Himself—Everything!
Much Love,
Swami VishwaVijayananda

Andra Pradesh And Telangana Pilgrimage, India
Join us on a very exciting and new sacred Pilgrimage with Paramahamsa Vishwananda as we explore East India, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana! This pilgrimage is meant for all who love and feel a special connection to the Lord Shri Narasimha Dev and Lord Rama.
We will be visiting numerous sacred places where the Lord manifested Himself to receive His blessings and protection. These holy shrines are famous and most loved and revered by all Vaishnavites.
Guruji particularly wanted to visit the Hindhu temples situated in Simhachalam as well as Bhadrachalam, dedicated to Narasimha Dev and Lord Rama. Simhachalam is one of the 18 Narasimha Dev kshetras, which is an important site to visit for all His devotees. We will also visit and have the opportunity with Guruji’s Grace to meet and receive blessing of some of the saints in the area.
But most importantly, we will be entering these sacred places along with our beloved Satguru Paramahamsa Vishwananda, whose Grace alone opens our awareness to the Divine, making this pilgrimage a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity where we experience an inward pilgrimage into our hearts. So come, enjoy and bathe yourself in the presence and Grace of the Master as we explore ancient India and, of course, ourselves! 
Check out the Pilgrimage information.
Holi Pilgrimage 2019 With Paramahamsa Vishwananda
Join us for an unforgettable pilgrimage with Paramahamsa Vishwananda for Holi celebration 2019 in the place where Radha and Krishna are playing it Themselves, in Vrindavan!
This is a COUNTRY PILGRIMAGE: only participants from Russia, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuvenia, Ukraine, Belorussia, Finland, and Denmark are welcome to join the most amazing and joyful festival of the year in Vrindavan with Paramahamsa Vishwananda!
For those of you who have participated in the Holi festivals with Gurudev before, there is no need to explain how amazing it is to play the colours with Lord Himself. But for those who have never been with Him in Vrindavan and especially for this amazing festival, we strongly recommend that you make the most of this rare opportunity!
In Vrindavan, Swami plays Holi with everyone for the whole week. We will participate in street kirtans and processions, take boat rides on the Majestic Yamuna River and go for special darshans in the beautiful and ancient temples of Vrindavan. Guruji has called Vrindavan “The Heart of the Universe”.
During this pilgrimage, our group will be staying at the Bhakti Marga Giridhar Dham Ashram, at the Jiva Institute (Vedic Studies) and at other ashrams located next door. A group from each country is asked to travel together, so that we can arrange a group transfer from Delhi to Vrindavan for you.
Don’t miss this absolutely precious chance of spending an unforgettable time in India with Paramahamsa Vishwananda! We would love all of you to fully participate and enjoy this incredible 10 days of celebration and the most bright, joyful and astonishing festival of the year in Vrindavan!
Check out the Pilgrimage information.
Diwali Govardhan Puja Kartik Night, India
The month of Kartik is one of the most beautiful and most celebrated in Vrindavan. It is in this month that Krishna played most of His leelas: Damodara Leela, Rasa Leela, Govardhan Leela. We will unfold and see all of them through the Grace of our Gurudev.
Paramahamsa Vishwananda will celebrate Diwali and Govardhan Puja, and dance throught the night of Kartik.
Register online: bhaktimarga.in.

Message of the Month
'Words are like sugar and the emotions and inner state is like a body of water. What happens to sugar when it comes in contact with water? It melts and disappears, but one can taste it through water. The water is still water, but it now contains the sweetness of the sugar. I think the best way to know what is in my heart is to taste the water from the oceans of my heart. Don’t be confused; this is not a salty sea in which sugar words will be lost. These are the cosmic oceans of Love within my heart.'
