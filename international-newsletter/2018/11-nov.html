NOVEMBER 2018, #43
God Longs For Us A Thousand Times More Than We Long For Him
Swami Tulsidasananda, SPN
One Monday morning, Swami Revatikaanta was giving a lecture to the residents. During this lecture, he shared a saint story that Guruji told him just a few days prior.
Two Sheiks once came to pay their respects to Saint Rabia. Both happened to be hungry, and they reflected, 'Whatever Rabia offers us will assuredly be permitted by Islamic law.' They seated themselves and she set two loaves of bread before them. At that moment, a beggar presented himself, asking for alms and Rabia gave him both loaves instead. Though both men were astounded at her behaviour, they made no objections. A maidservant then entered Rabia’s cottage with an armful of warm bread.
'My mistress has sent these for you,' she explained. Rabia took them and counted eighteen loaves, 'There must be some error,' she remarked. 'Please take them back to your mistress.' Though the girl protested, Rabia insisted they be returned.
When the maidservant brought the loaves back to her mistress and recounted the story, the lady supplemented her gift by two additional loaves. This time when the girl brought the loaves to Rabia, the saint acknowledged the gift of twenty loaves. Rabia laid the loaves before her guests and, though surprised, they enjoyed the meal.
Afterwards, they ventured to ask Saint Rabia the mystery of the events they had witnessed. 'As you entered,' she said, 'I saw you both were hungry, but I felt it wouldn't be becoming to offer such notables as yourselves so meager a fare as two loaves. Therefore, I bestowed them on the beggar, entreating, 'O God, you have promised to repay our charity tenfold. This is beyond doubt. I have offered You two loaves; grant me a tenfold return then.' When the maidservant brought only eighteen, I saw that a mistake had occurred, or else they had been incorrectly delivered to me. I returned them until the proper amount I had petitioned for arrived.'
As Swami Revatikaanta finished the story everyone listening gave a universal sigh of love--except me. While Swami went on about the importance of faith in God and how God reflects our love, I kept thinking 'What a stingy God! Only ten times? That is not true. My God and Guru gives a thousand times, a million times, infinite times more in return. I know this because Guruji and Tulsi have proven it to me.'
Some weeks later Guruji was leading a meditation in Babaji cave where we visualized the Feet of our Guru in our heart. Everyone was in such a bhav, enjoying the closeness of the Guru inside and out. In the middle of the meditation Guruji said, 'Whatever love you are projecting to your Guru, know He is reflecting ten times, one hundred times back to you.' Immediately the bhav left me. I was again upset.
I thought, 'Why are you lying? I know it is more than that! Ugh. I can’t understand why You would say such things, Guruji.' Then I thought, 'No, come on Tulsidas. If Guruji says it is ten times or a hundred times then it is ten times or a hundred times. What do you know, Tulsidas? Nothing. Don’t be a bad disciple and…' But then again, 'I know it is more than just one hundred times.'
My mind jumped back and forth like this for the rest of the meditation. It was awful. Not only that, I couldn't get it out of my mind for the next weeks. All the time I was arguing with myself about how much God and Guru give back to us.
One evening I was walking out of my room when I saw Guruji. I ran to touch His Feet and he asks me, 'Tulsidas, how are you?' 'Good,' I say. 'Good, can you come with me? I need your help,' Guruji said with a smile. Excitedly I say, 'Yes of course!'
Guruji and I walked into His bungalow and He led me to His room. He picks up one of His freshly washed dhotis and hands me one side of it. 'I need your help folding my dhoti.' I think, 'That’s not true. If I can fold my dhotis, You can fold Yours. You do not need my help.'
But it was obvious why He asked me to 'help' Him. Guruji was using it as an excuse to spend a few moments with me. So I whispered, not loud enough for Guruji to physically hear me, 'I love you.' He ripped the dhoti out of my hands and yelled, 'I love you too! Ten times. Hundred times. Thousand times. Whatever you want, I love you.'
God will reflect whatever relationship we want. But if we want a pure relationship, then we must also be pure; hence, Atma Kriya Yoga.
When Guruji says, 'Whatever love you are projecting to your Guru, know He is reflecting ten times, one hundred times back to you,' He is not just talking about a feeling, but in every way possible.
Much Love,
Swami Tulsidasananda

Special East India Pilgrimage
Don’t miss this rare opportunity to visit some of the most sacred places in India with Paramahamsa Vishwananda.
Join us on a very exciting pilgrimage with Paramahamsa Vishwananda as we explore East India, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana!
This pilgrimage is meant for all who love and feel a special connection to the Lord Sri Narasimha Dev and Lord Rama.
We will be visiting several sacred places where the Lord manifested Himself so that we can receive His blessings and protection. These holy shrines are famous and are the most loved and revered by all Vaishnavas. 
Guruji particularly wanted to visit the Hindu temples situated in Simhachalam as well as Bhadrachalam that are dedicated to Narasimha Dev and Lord Rama. Simhachalam is one of the 18 Narasimha Dev kshetras, which is an important site to visit for all His devotees. We will also visit and have the opportunity with Guruji’s Grace to meet and receive the blessing of some of the saints in the area.
But most importantly, we will be entering these sacred places along with our beloved Satguru Paramahamsa Vishwananda, whose Grace alone opens our awareness to the Divine, making this pilgrimage a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity where we experience an inward pilgrimage into our hearts. So come, enjoy and bathe yourself in the presence and Grace of the Master as we explore ancient India and, of course, ourselves!
Register now: Registration closes 30 November.

Holi Pilgrimage 2019 with Paramahamsa Vishwananda, India
Join us for an unforgettable pilgrimage with Paramahamsa Vishwananda for Holi celebration 2019 in the place where Radha and Krishna are playing it Themselves, in Vrindavan!
This is a COUNTRY PILGRIMAGE: only participants from Russia, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Ukraine, Belorussia, Finland, and Denmark are welcome to join the most amazing and joyful festival of the year in Vrindavan with Paramahamsa Vishwananda!
For those of you who have participated in the Holi festivals with Gurudev before, there is no need to explain how amazing it is to play the colours with Lord Himself. But for those who have never been with Him in Vrindavan and especially for this amazing festival, we strongly recommend that you make the most of this rare opportunity!
In Vrindavan, Swami plays Holi with everyone for the whole week. We will participate in street kirtans and processions, take boat rides on the majestic Yamuna River and go for special darshans in the beautiful and ancient temples of Vrindavan. Guruji has called Vrindavan “The Heart of the Universe”.
During this pilgrimage, our group will be staying at the Bhakti Marga Giridhar Dham Ashram, at the Jiva Institute (Vedic Studies) and at other ashrams located next door. A group from each country is asked to travel together, so that we can arrange a group transfer from Delhi to Vrindavan for you.
Don’t miss this absolutely precious chance of spending an unforgettable time in India with Paramahamsa Vishwananda! We would love all of you to fully participate and enjoy this incredible 10 days of celebration and the most bright, joyful and astonishing festival of the year in Vrindavan!

Message of the Month
'On the bhakti path, we cannot 'do' bhakti. The same with Love; we cannot 'do' Love. We can only surrender, and be fully involved in the present moment and in the remembrance of the Lord, and things happen on their own. In one moment, one can have an insight, but one cannot do it. That is Bhakti: one can experience it, but not do it. It is Love that will take us, will reach us, and not the other way around. That is the reason why we on the Bhakti path are talking about building the relationship with God: on the bhakti path, all depends on the ‘building’. Building the relationship, the energy, and that is why we sing and dance. It is building the energy and if we are in kirtan with our full heart and full involvement, that is all we can do.
