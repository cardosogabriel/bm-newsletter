DECEMBER 2018, #44
Selfless Service – True Seva
Swami Vishwaanashuyananda, SPN
This time of year is an important reminder, as we can see how much people are drawn to the materialistic world. Whichever country you live in, mostly Christmas starts already in October - all the shops are filling up their windows with the Christmas stock and marketing things to buy. It‘s a far cry from the real meaning of Christmas. I am sure Jesus would not have encouraged His disciples to purchase computer games and excessive things, and to eat until one is nearly ready to explode! But I am sure that He would have suggested to return to simplicity and the true meaning of life.
It is interesting how many people use gifts to bring out this happiness from inside another person, but we should remember that this happiness – if attached to gifts, food, etc. – will always be temporary and not really give us that transformation of consciousness we desire. It is this selfless service – true seva, truly giving of ourselves with love – that allows this unfolding of the heart to reveal this Divine Light, so that it can radiate and illuminate not just our own life, but also the lives of others, all those beloved people around us. Such great examples are Mahavatar Babaji, who decided to stay on the Earth and help all humanity to attain the Divine, and Saint Nicholas who was also the embodiment of selfless service as charity. They are such great examples of charity that their cups of divine abundance overflowed! Their generosity of giving could not be limited since they both had entered into that stream of Divine Grace and Divine Love. We will commemorate Babaji day on 30 November and St Nicholas on 7 December here in SPN.
It’s a great wonder to know that these Masters are always ready to serve if one remembers to ask for their help sincerely. I leave you with a short miracle about St Nicholas which took place in Andros, Greece several years ago. Every year on St. Nicholas' Feast Day, Abbot Dorotheos gives out little packets of blessed wheat from the litany in honour of the saint. A few years ago, just as he had given out the last packet, a local fisherman came in and hurried to the altar to receive his wheat. Instead, the abbot gave him a small bottle of oil from the lamp hanging over the saint's icon. The fisherman put it in his coat pocket and left. A few weeks later, he was at sea when a sudden storm arose. Although he was a skilled sailor, the storm became violent and, unable to get to shore, he feared that his boat would capsize. He began to pray to St. Nicholas, and remembering the holy oil, took the bottle from his pocket and poured it into the sea. Immediately the wind ceased, and in a short time the water around the boat was as smooth as glass. The fisherman sailed back to Andros, giving thanks for the saint's intercession.
Hope to see you soon for these festivals or Christmas.
Much love to you all and keep up with your most precious seva!
Sw Vishwaanashuyananda

Holi Pilgrimage 2019 with Paramahamsa Vishwananda, India
Join us for an unforgettable pilgrimage with Paramahamsa Vishwananda for Holi celebration 2019 in the place where Radha and Krishna are playing it Themselves, in Vrindavan!
This is a COUNTRY PILGRIMAGE: only participants from Russia, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Ukraine, Belorussia, Finland, and Denmark are welcome to join the most amazing and joyful festival of the year in Vrindavan with Paramahamsa Vishwananda!
For those of you who have participated in the Holi festivals with Gurudev before, there is no need to explain how amazing it is to play the colours with Lord Himself. But for those who have never been with Him in Vrindavan and especially for this amazing festival, we strongly recommend that you make the most of this rare opportunity!
In Vrindavan, Swami plays Holi with everyone for the whole week. We will participate in street kirtans and processions, take boat rides on the majestic Yamuna River and go for special darshans in the beautiful and ancient temples of Vrindavan. Guruji has called Vrindavan “The Heart of the Universe”.
During this pilgrimage, our group will be staying at the Bhakti Marga Giridhar Dham Ashram, at the Jiva Institute (Vedic Studies) and at other ashrams located next door. A group from each country is asked to travel together, so that we can arrange a group transfer from Delhi to Vrindavan for you.
Don’t miss this absolutely precious chance of spending an unforgettable time in India with Paramahamsa Vishwananda! We would love all of you to fully participate and enjoy this incredible 10 days of celebration and the most bright, joyful and astonishing festival of the year in Vrindavan!

5 Elements Retreat
Join Paramahamsa Vishwananda for an inner and outer journey on Lanzarote, the ‘Island of Transformation’. In this unique environment, you will explore the power of the five elements, both in yourself and out in nature.
This meditative retreat offers an opportunity to take a deep dive into the ancient knowledge of secret mantras and mudras that can help you better manage the effects of Earth, Water, Fire, Air and Ether in your own body.
Take advantage of this rare time away from your busy life to deepen your meditation practice under the extraordinary guidance of Paramahamsa Vishwananda. During this unforgettable week, you’ll enjoy hours of daily meditation out in the nature of this ruggedly beautiful volcanic island. You’ll also be treated to stunning views, vegan meals, hikes, and a swim in the ocean.
All explanations of the elements, mantras and mudras will be given only in English. Please note, translations are not available, so you must be able to speak and understand English to participate in this retreat.
