True Meaning of Christmas
Swami Vishwaanashuyananda, Shree Peetha Nilaya
Jai Gurudev,
This Christmas will be certainly a different Christmas than what people have experienced before. The whole Christmas event is pinned around the birth of Christ. But strangely, this is not really featured these days. What people really associate as being Christmas is eating and drinking, getting or giving presents and being with family or partying.
Most people around the world do not even actually know who Santa Claus is! I have travelled to many countries and have sometimes asked this question. Many people have no clue that Santa Claus is based on one of Guruji’s favourite saints, Saint Nicholas Bishop of Myra in Lycia. (Lycia is now modern-day Turkey.) In those days, Saint Nicholas did not have reindeer and he certainly didn’t have any elves or a sled that he flew around the world in 24 hours. But he was the personification of charity. He was always caring, kind-hearted and giving. The greatest way in which he would give would be to give his love to anyone who was in need.
In this way, he was a perfect example of dharma – one who would always be giving – his time, his capacity to help and his love. Even after death, he continues to appear and intercede for thousands of people around the world. He has performed thousands of miracles worldwide with his intercession. He is known as the ‘speedy intercessor’, meaning he quickly answers prayers.
Much love,
Swami Anashuya
