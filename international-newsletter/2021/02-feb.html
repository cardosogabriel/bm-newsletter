WHEN IN DOUBT, DO JAPA

Swami VishwaKeshavaAnanda, SPN

Jai Gurudev everyone,

Reflecting on what's most important and fruitful (not only now but in life in general) I would like to share a verse from the story of Ajamila in the Srimad Bhagavatam with you.

 

'One who chants the holy name of the Lord is immediately freed from the reactions of unlimited sins, even if he chants indirectly [to indicate something else], jokingly, for musical entertainment, or even neglectfully. This is accepted by all the learned scholars of the scriptures.' (Srimad Bhagavatam, 6.2.14)

 

Guruji so often speaks about, and even more, continuously shows us with His actions, the importance, power and beauty of the Lord's name. Especially on the spiritual path, we strive for high goals which are not in our control or reach. In doing so, we forget or overlook the easiest and most important things which are right in front of us - and that most definitely includes His Name.

 

Remembering His Name will not only purify us, it will not only change our life but more than anything else it brings joy and happiness. His Name is infused with this deep unexplainable peace, joy and freedom. Doing our best to simply remember Him as often as we can is all the effort we need to and can do. Everything else is given by Him through grace when we are ready.

 

Much Love,

Swami Keshava

PARAMAHAMSA VISHWANANDA

MESSAGE OF THE MONTH

Q: How do you fully focus your mind on God if you choose to live as grihastha? Can you elaborate on how to focus your mind on you with so many cares?

 

You should engage your whole family in service of guru and God. Then you will never be able to forget about guru and God. If you are just doing it, then it is difficult, because you will have to care so much about everything else. But if you engage your family into the service, if you are practising, you engage your family, also to your kids, you start to teach your kids from being small to read the Bhagavad Gita, to understand the Bhagavad Gita, then there will be not a hardship in remembering guru and God.

 

But if you have not yourself been practising that, continuous remembering of God, what do you expect? It will be easy? No, it will not be easy.

 

Yesterday, it was so sweet, Ukelele sent me a beautiful video. So, he sent me a beautiful video of Manjari doing puja. And she was chanting her own mantra with the udharini and all these stuff. But it's so sweet, actually, because you see from the beginning there is this tradition, this culture. It's not about running behind culture or tradition, but this is who you are. This is my path, I belong to that path. And of course, I have to share it with my child. No? And that child will grow into that.
