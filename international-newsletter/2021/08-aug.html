ISLAND OF HIS LOVE

Swami Paranthapa, South Africa

Islands, by their very nature, are unique and incredibly vulnerable. Life on islands often evolve with refined specialities to ensure the survival of the fittest. Often the food and life cycles are very stark displaying the genius of Mother Nature. They are places that conjure up spectacular sunsets, a leisurely pace and picturesque palm trees swaying in the wind. 

 

This imagery beautifully describes the magical time I recently spent with Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda on the Island of Mauritius. It felt to me like a juxtaposition of the human story in the mundane world and the life lived for the purpose of the ultimate realisation playing out next to each other.

 

Having the proclivity to watch, I watched the Master playing on His island home from as many different perspectives as I could muster. Walking behind Him in local markets, dancing and watching Him dance during the celebrations attended, and weathering a cyclone in His care all abbreviated by the laughter of the Mauritian devotees in response to His jokes in Creole. 

 

Islands are indeed unique, hiding as they do, so many treasures. In being there with Guruji this time and seeing His unique yet consistent compassionate and loving actions regardless of whether we notice or appreciate them, I began to see that we are not just on the island of Mauritius but for all intents we are on the island of His Love.

 

He coaxes us into the possibility that life can be a life of love lived for love in love and does so by demonstration, not just with words. I watched so many responses - many loving, some confused, shocked and even some relief on the faces of others as they interacted with Him. None of this changed His loving attentiveness. Dusks and dawns, like crest-jewels, accentuated the beauty of all the moments spent together. 


The highlight of this time for me was every evening after the abhishekam of Giridhari. We got to sit in Guruji’s satsang where He responded to questions asked by people. I am fascinated that He brings every answer back to the truth of Love’s eternal call from within each of us to come home, that this is the easiest and most natural thing to do. The most striking for me was the question ‘What is life?’ and the answer ‘Life is service.’ From this island time with my Lord, I learned I am on the island of His Love, in the ocean of His Love and if I am to drown, there is no better substance to do it in but His love and that is His service to each one.

 

Much love,

Swami Paranthapa
