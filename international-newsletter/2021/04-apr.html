NEWSLETTER #72 · Apr 2021

THE GRACE AND LOVE OF GURUJI

Swami Revatikānta, SPN

Jai Gurudev dear Bhakti Marga family,

In the past month or so we have been graced with an abundance of satsang time with our beloved Guruji. Almost every day, He has taken the time to teach us, to answer our questions and to simply be with us. As a result, we have been immersed in the forms of bhakti known as shravana and kirtana – hearing and speaking of the Lord and His glories. Through this virtual communion, Guruji has masterfully arranged it so that by His doing, we continue our spiritual development and practice, with minimal effort required on our side.

Several of our sanghas around the world are currently experiencing unavoidable obstacles in coming together, be it to practice kirtan, to engage in temple worship, to discuss the holy scriptures or any other group sadhana. At the same time, many of us are struggling with motivation and self-discipline to practice our personal sadhana, such as japa, meditation and prayer to the level we would ideally envision for ourselves. This drop is understandable given how much mental willpower it takes to remain positive and functional in these times of isolation, uncertainty and restriction. But no matter how much we may empathise with each other, this fall in discipline and motivation can be very detrimental to our spiritual progress. The mind is the mind – it cares not for a pandemic, for governmental restrictions, changes to our external conditions and the like. It will always pose the same challenges, manifest the same tendencies and obstruct our spiritual progress until we subdue it and transform it. When we stop working on that, when we stop counteracting the influence of maya, then maya pushes us back, undoing much of our previous good work. But I am not writing this to instil fear in anyone, but rather to better showcase the greatness of what Guruji is doing on our behalf.

In the 37th verse of the Narada-bhakti-sutra, Sage Narada states that ‘one develops bhakti by hearing and singing the glories of the Lord, even while engaged in the ordinary activities of the world.’ This is precisely what Guruji is facilitating for us. We must never forget that the guru willingly accepts responsibility for the spiritual well-being of His children. In this moment in time, nothing displays this care more clearly than the frequency with which He is offering Himself to us, and the relative ease with which we can accept that offering. He sees our struggle, He sees our need, and He rises to meet it every single time. All that we are required to do is to log on to our devices and simply listen to the satsang. That simple act will ensure the continued development of our bhakti, irrespective of our worldly engagements, as declared by Sage Narada. We need only listen, we need only ‘show up’ and once again we will be advancing, moving further from maya and closer to the Lord’s heart. I am certain that you, just as I am, are extremely grateful to Guruji for this mercy. But gratitude is always best acted upon and not merely stated – so my parting thought is this: the same way Guruji is nourishing and protecting us during these tough times, help nourish and protect someone else by connecting them to Guruji. In the Bhagavatam, Prahlad instructs his less-fortunate demon friends, to ‘show mercy to all living entities by enlightening them in devotional service, thus displaying them true friendship’. We may not be able to enlighten them, but we sure can point them in the right direction.

 

Much love,
Swami Revatikānta

Create Your Own Happiness

'That source of happiness is not outside, it’s inside of you. Stop looking, stop being a foolish person thinking that somebody else will make you happy. No one will ever make you happy. You make yourself happy when you choose to be happy. The moment you think that somebody else will make you happy, you will become miserable, you will become unhappy. The moment you think that ‘If I have this thing or that, I will be happy,’ you will be the most miserable person. Dive within and find that true happiness which the Supreme Lord Sriman Narayana has placed inside of you.'
