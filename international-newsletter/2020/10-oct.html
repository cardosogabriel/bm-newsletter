These changes were inspired by a conversation held between Guruji and a few devotees in which He expressed a developing tendency that He had observed in the mission. He said that the devotees are too keen to ‚Äòpeople-please‚Äô. In other words, we try to say and do what we think others want to hear or see from us, rather than what we authentically wish to say or do.
In order to address this, at least partly, we concluded that we need to be more direct and clearer in our communication, with the hope that this approach will eventually trickle down into the mindset of all the devotees. So, what does that mean?
Simply put, Bhakti Marga is only about the pursuit of God-realisation through the grace of Guruji. Everything we do ‚Äì be it meditations, pujas, kirtans, studying, painting, etc. ‚Äì is done solely with this aim in mind. Therefore, given that all our practices share the same aim, why not bring them closer together? It is this line of thinking that has led us to the formation of what we term as the ‚Äòfour arms‚Äô of Bhakti Marga‚Äôs teachings. The many practices of BM have been simplified and grouped into four distinct categories, replacing the previous hubs of ‚ÄòSadhana‚Äô and the ‚ÄòAcademy‚Äô.
These arms are:
‚Ä® - The arm of Knowledge for all our scriptural and philosophical endeavours.
 - The arm of Yoga & Meditation, containing Atma Kriya Yoga, OM Chanting and other yogic practices.
‚Ä® - The arm of Ritual, containing all our pujas as well as Vedic chanting.
 - The arm of Devotional Arts, where we can find music, dance and art, co-existing.
These four arms are designed by Guruji to work together in order to enrich the devotee‚Äôs life in a holistic manner. Viewing them in isolation, as islands, is certainly not Guruji‚Äôs intention. As a practical example, this means that when we teach Kriya, we should speak about the Bhagavad Gita and our wider philosophy. When we teach the Gita, we should encourage students to take up devotional practices, such as puja and kirtan. And at the core of all this stands Guruji. He is the reason all of these arms work. We firmly believe that it is only by His grace that our spiritual aspirations may be realised and therefore, the most authentic thing for us to do is to communicate that.
It was truly beautiful to see how much time Guruji chose to spend with the group and with each one individually. He went for multiple walks with the group, taking the time to speak with each one individually.
Naturally, we must be grounded, respectful and understanding ‚Äì the last thing Guruji wants is a fanatical crowd, flying in the clouds, proclaiming Guruji‚Äôs divinity! But by that same token, neither does He wish for a family that is too afraid or too concerned with their reputation to share the most important thing in their lives with the world.
Sadly, I must stop here! This is just the tip of the iceberg in many ways. There are several changes brewing in the background, but rather than explain them all in detail, I felt it was best to explain Guruji‚Äôs intentions behind these changes. After all, aligning ourselves with Him is, and will always be, the most important thing we can do.
