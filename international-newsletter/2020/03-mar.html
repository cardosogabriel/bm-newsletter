MARCH 2020, #59
God is Everywhere
Swami Vishwaaniruddhananda, SPN
“You are Atiti for us, you are God for us, please accept our offerings!”…like this I was asked when I visited a private home in India around noon. The mother was having her afternoon nap on her bed, the daughter was cleaning the house, somebody was cleaning up and others were doing other duties. A normal day in an Indian household. The only thing different was that suddenly a white Swami together with two other Indian companions were visiting, and inquiries were made about the saint who may live there or nearby. (We found the information by searching Google.)
I always imagine how it is when we visit people in Europe. You normally call, make an appointment, and definitely you try not to disturb them during the wrong times. Private times, personal times. Privacy...? not sure that exists in the Indian vocabulary! In fact, we didn’t call, didn’t make any appointment, came at the “wrong” time when people have their duties to fulfill or when they can rest, and instead of being in any way upset with our visit, they put everything aside.
First, they offered us a seat. Then some water. Then a tea or coffee. Then they brought the prasad and make sure we got some food. It was obvious that they were not expecting anybody and that we were creating work for them. They were preparing everything in the moment. He asked, ‘Can we offer you some more food?’ and I say: ‘No, thank you! Actually, we are sorry if we created any inconvenience!!’ ‘Inconvenience?’ he answered back… ’No, no. You are atiti for us! Atiti devo bhava. Guest is God.’ His eyes were shining and he kept asking us to take and accept something more.
What a way of seeing reality! God is everywhere at all times, and the purpose is to share our love with Him, through service... every moment is a good moment. So simple it can be, because we truly never know in which form God is standing next to us. This attitude I saw in almost every environment I experienced during this pilgrimage through Maharashtra. It reminded me always of Guruji and His way of caring for the devotees and guests which come to His home.
Now I am back in SPN and enjoying the sangha. I joined the Sangha Facilitator Training and we shared a lot on the point of remembering God all the time, and the fact that He is seated in all. I see that this is a really great tool to become a better human being, a better devotee, and it’s a constant reminder that helps to intensify the love relationship with God/ guru, who is seated in all. If we take this to heart, we will definitely reach the long sought-after satisfaction of enjoying His presence constantly.
Atiti devo bhava - guest is God
Acharya devo bhava – guru is God
Jai Gurudev
Swami Vishwaaniruddhananda

Message of the Month
Jai Gurudev! Here you have Kripanidhe Sri Ramchandra Bhagavan, the Lord of mercy. He is holding in His hand the bow and the right hand is giving the blessing. He is dark in colour because He is everything. His eyes are very merciful.
Next to Him is His wife, Sita-devi, and to His right is Lakshman. Below is Hanumanji, and Hanumanji is so beautiful, looking at his beloved Lord. Then you have Ram Darbar as a Shaligram, which comes from Nepal. In this Shaligram, you have Rama and Sita sitting on the throne. At His feet is Hanuman, Narada, Narad Rishi, Brahma, Ganeshji, Shankar Bhagavan. You have the two brothers, Bharat and Shatrughna. And Lakshmana. And Valmiki is there also. In front is the padam of Sri Rama and in front of Sita is the padam of Sita.
