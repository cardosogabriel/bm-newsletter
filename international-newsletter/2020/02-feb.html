FEBRUARY 2020, #58
Relationship Between Lord and Devotees
Swami VishwaParanthapananda, Shree Peetha Nilaya
In a Satsang recently given during the latter part of the Maharashtra pilgrimage, Guruji presented a reality that I have not considered, at least not in the context that He mentioned it this time. It has always been clear to me that the Lord is continuously rendering service and that this is always colored by Love for the sake of the progress of the collective.
What really struck me is the absolute certainty of the Lord’s love for those who love Him. The saints we visited were giants towering over their contemporaries. In Vrindavan, everyone goes to see the Lord, but here in Maharashtra, the Lord comes to His devotees. Tukaram, Ekanath, Naamdev and Jana Bhai are only a few examples of the love relationship between Him and His devotees. The Lord enters the life of the saint where they are and turns whatever seems simple and normal into a miraculous display that inspires humans to reach for something more than the material.
This got me thinking that this is not different from where any of us are. We are looking for a way to see the reality of God’s presence in our lives, but this would be really difficult to do by ourselves. When the time is right, Guruji comes into our lives and finds us, just as Tukaram and Eknath where found by their gurus. Like this, Guruji always finds us and presents the same opportunity to each of us. In the marketplace of instant sense gratification, it is the rare chance to be in a relationship with Love.
Swami Paranthapa

Message of the Month
Happy New Year 2020! May Lord Nrsingadev protect all of you. May Srimati Radharani graces you all with Her Love so that you can get closer in your devotion to the Supreme Lord Himself.
Every year, people say they want to change something. Very often we say, ‘What you have to change is your mind’. We forget that in Chapter 10, Verse 2, Krishna said, ‘Among all the senses, I am the mind.’ That mind itself is Krishna. When He said, ‘Among the senses, I am the mind’, it means that your mind must be focused upon Him. If your mind is focused upon Him, everything in your life will be perfect. But if your mind is diverted from Him, this is where a problem arises.
