JANUARY 2020, #57
The Importance of Sangha and Sharing of His Glory
Swami Kurunandha, SPN
Jai Gurudev, dear family in Guruji’s Love,
As I’m writing these lines on Christmas Eve, and I’m contemplating on the scene around the birth of Jesus Christ, I see two messages that I want to share:
1. The importance of Sangha. Christmas has always been a festival for coming together, sharing, and the joy that comes with being together with true family. I remember when I was young and my grandmother gathered the whole family in one place, it was always a special joyous moment, full of Light and Love. This is what we experience now even more as we deepen our connection to our ultimate family in God, the eternal spiritual family of our sweet Guruji - perhaps His greatest gift to all of us. Recently He confirmed what we felt intuitively - that the Sangha is Him! May we carry this insight in our hearts, as we progress on our spiritual path, hand in hand with our family members, realising that what Christ said, and Guruji so often repeats: ‘Where two or three are gathered in my Name, there I am with them.’ This is so true. May 2020 be remembered as the year in which many new BM Sanghas all across the world were born and began to flourish.
2. The importance of sharing His Glory. The shepherds who assembled at the cradle firstly, and later many more chosen ones who had the privilege of meeting the Satguru named Jesus (because that’s what He was in truth, a Divine Guru), happily shared about their experience. We ought to do the same. Most of our incredible experiences with our Divine Guru are not to be hidden. They are to be shared with the whole world. This is our most important gift back to Him who is Love itself, who graced us with its presence. That is what the birth of Jesus, of the Guru and God Consciousness inside our hearts stands for. In reality, there were many more “gospels”, not only four, as each disciple of Jesus wrote down his or her experiences. By sharing, we cultivate not only gratitude, we touch others’ lives and we multiply the blessings contained in the Divine experience. May 2020 become the year where sharing our experiences about Guruji’s incredible Love started to truly reach those who are ready and long for it.
Together these two messages for 2020 are like “20/20” eyesight: we begin to see what’s truly important, and we make it happen, together as one.
I wish you from the bottom of my heart a most Happy New year!
Much Love,
Swami Kurunandha

A yogi functions completely differently. Somebody asked me once, ‘Is Babaji a Vaishnava or is He Saivite? Is He an Advaitist? Is He Advaita Dvaita? What is He?’ This is very often in the minds of people, because when we say certain things about a yogi, you think that, yes, a yogi is somebody who is beyond duality. Like Lord Krishna said to Arjuna in Chapter 6, Verse 46: ‘A yogi is greater than an ascetic, greater than a man of knowledge, greater than a man of work, karma yoga.’ He urged Arjuna, ‘Become a yogi.’

Bhajamana Sri Radhe by Aradhana
‘Bhajamane Sri Radhe’ is a devotional combination of traditional kirtans set in a western rock groove. Lively with soaring melodies, this album naturally draws us in to chanting along and becomes an addictive, soul-stirring form of spiritual practice.
The great devotional saints declare that to chant the names of God is to actually have God. It is to deliver the transcendent realm to our mind and heart. Each one is a different gateway to the same abode. When we sing the name of ‘Radha’ we awaken that ecstatic longing of a lover for their beloved. When we call upon ‘Vitthala’, we approach Him as a rustic simpleton eager to see the Divine as our nearest and dearest. When we chant ‘Narayana’, we recognise the highest, all-encompassing glory of God.
