DEC 2020, #68
Kartik Month
Swami Vishwaaniruddhananda, SPN
Jai Gurudev,
Auspicious times come as a reminder. After we celebrated the Divine Mother for 9 nights, the holy month for Lord Vishnu, Kartik, started. While all these beautiful events remind us of the essence in life, the world around us focuses more and more on the transitory things and we get caught up in the drama of it. Winter is coming, a second lockdown is happening. The whole world seems to go again into a phase of purification.
The Ashram has also adapted and made measures, giving all the residents time to rest and deepen their relationship with God. After all, it is our spiritual path which is related to a lasting truth; everything else is transitory. Coming and going. Like Guruji always says: in every good is something bad and in every bad something good. It really depends what we focus on. Guruji, the path and the sangha help us to remember what really counts, and to focus on the opportunities that life give us.
The beauty is that through modern media, with livestreams and online meetings, we can be even more connected than we were before. But, still, we must watch out. Easily we can get caught up in the matters of this world. Sometimes things seem to be very urgent or very tragic and beyond our abilities to deal with. But that is only because we limit ourselves and our perspectives.
We speak about our spiritual heritage, we speak about the importance of calming the mind and not entering into the drama of the world, but how many really make a conscious effort to reach that state? By that, I mean not just practising mechanically the instruments Guruji has given us, but really making a conscious effort to become better, dive deeper and live more the eternal truths which we hear and speak so often.
It will not happen without a continuous conscious effort. Last time I asked Guruji how to overcome our doubts and fears, and why so many people lose track of their path, He answered, ‘You have to declare war on this mind when she gets crazy.’ Guruji and God want to see that we are sincere in our quest. They want to see how we pick up our bow and fight this sacred fight against our mind, ego and the desires and limitations that spring from them.
So let’s take a deep breath with the name of God while picturing the image of our guru, and welcome the prospect of a new awakening and deepening of the experience of ourselves and of our love relationship with the Eternal One. We need to be strong and endure!
Much love,
Swami Aniruddha
