JUN 2020, #62
Be the Observer of the Mind
Swami Pepe, SPN
Truly, the Satguru is the blessing that we received after so many lives of sadhana. So let's rejoice for this chance that we have in this life to know Him and to build our connection with Him.
Let's follow His advice, not missing a moment to think about Him, to think about Giridhariji, about God, about the Divine in us. Let's remember, if only for just a few seconds during the day, that we are divine, that we are a part of Him, that He came for us on this earth to give the most precious gift that all souls wish for.
In May 2009, we were discussing at the table of the dining room in the bungalow:
I said, 'We often identify ourselves with the mind.'
Guruji answered: 'You must become an observer of the mind and observe your thoughts. But most people run after their mind. They take what their mind produces as the truth and act without testing their thinking. Everything that comes into their heads goes out through their mouths. They speak whatever the mind produces. Consciousness has the role to observe the thoughts and deeds. When we meditate, the observer can watch the mind.' 'Meditating calms the mind?'
'When you calm the mind and then you talk little, you become the observer of every moment. And when you observe your mind constantly, you can connect with your heart and the heart is connected with the Divine.' 'When we pray, what is the highest prayer?'
'This is the prayer of silence: when we talk and we pray to God within oneself. When we pray in silence, it is the observer who prays.'
I said, 'The external world is often too attractive even if we know that it is an illusion.'
Guruji answered, 'With your five senses you can enjoy the garden, but with your heart you discover the gardener who is even more beautiful than the garden.'
Much love,
Swami Pepe
