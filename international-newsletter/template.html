JULY 2016 Edition, #16
Issued at Shree Peetha Nilaya, Springen/Germany
The objective for all of us depending on what we want is very clear. 125,000 generations of people have come and gone on this planet and very few in this time have realized the nearness of the love that is God. We celebrate so many things that bring us only temporary happiness. We seldom have the chance to come together and celebrate Love.
That is what the Just Love festival is about. It is about celebrating what is the nearest and dearest to us disguised as our true self. All of this in the presence of one who is the living embodiment of that Divine Love, Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda. What becomes very clear is that when we come together in the presence of Love, we grow in Love and it happens spontaneously because that is what He is and what He wants for us each.
That is why He works tirelessly for all of us, so by being here for whatever event, we join hands with Him and one another honoring the purpose that we came here, which is to Love and by doing so, many things fall into place. We become part of the solution simply by putting Love first.
As Guruji says "Just Love, Just do it" and we each can.
Swami Paranthapa, South Africa
BM Around the World
· Events with Swami Baladhara in Eastern Bohemia, Czech Republic: Readings out of Just Love and Blossoming of the Heart, bhajans and abishekam to Maha Lakshmi and Narasimha, general sharing of experiences with Guruji and much more took place.
· Yagna in Graz Austria with Swami Kurunandha: More than 20 devotees participated in the yagna which was offered to Shri Lakshmi Narayana.
· Cooking for Guruji course in Bhajan Cafe in Riga, Latvia: Over 10 participants learned with Mataji Lakshmi how to prepare delicious prasad and dishes which Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda loves to eat. If you want to learn to cook Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda’s favourite meals, please contact your country coordinator.
·Devotees worldwide gathered in most of the BM groups and temples worldwide to celebrate the appearance day of Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda.
· Atma Kriya Yoga course with Swami Paranthapa: In South Africa, Swami Paranthapa gave an Atma Kriya Yoga course and initiated 15 new sadhaks.
· Swamini Prabhaavati in Soria, Spain: Swamini travels tirelessly to different cities of Spain, this time to Soria where she spreads the Love and knowledge of the Just Love & Sri Guru Gita books.
· Swamini Lakshmi visits Czech Republic: Swamini Lakshmi visited Swami Baladhara and the Czech devotees to celebrate Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda’s appearance day – more than 130 devotees attended a Guru puja, Satsang, OM Chanting, Sudarshana yagna, received Vishnu Padam blessings and offered as a birthday gift the national heart-shaped linden tree.
· Country Darshan of UK, Canada and USA at Shree Peetha Nilaya: Over 95 devotees from UK and 9 devotees from Canada and USA were blessed with Darshan and Satsang with Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda.
· Music Workshop in Helsinki, Finland: Aaradhakananda from Shree Peetha Nilaya travelled to Helsinki where he taught more than 20 devotees how to play harmonium, mridanga and to sing bhajans.
· Pre-event in Ticino, South Switzerland, with Swamini VishwaKishori Ma: Swamini made a presentation about “Love as the key for good health and a joyful living”, there was OM Chanting, a satsang, bhajans and a powerful Lakshmi Yagna.

Sri Swami Vishwananda  @vishwananda   .  24 Jun   
There was a great #saint of Bengal, #Yukteshwar Giri. He was known as a great renunciate. A #wealthy person once came to him and said, ”You are a great renunciate.” Giri laughed out loud. ”Look at him!” he said, pointing to the man. ”Look at him! He is a great renunciate himself, and he is calling me one. Don’t try to trap me with your words, my good man!” Everyone was shocked, even his #disciples. They begged him to explain because there was no doubt that Giri was a great #renunciate. Giri said, ”Suppose there is a pile of #diamonds and a pile of pebbles. This man holds on the pebbles, while I hold on the diamonds! And he thinks I am a renunciate!” Who has renounced, Mahavir or you, #Buddha or you? You have renounced, for you hold on to rubbish. You have discarded the joy of samadhi in favor of the #anxiety-ridden affairs of the #periphery, and what have you got in exchange for your bliss is so flimsy. So coarse and crude! So stale and dirty! The #worldly man is a great renunciate, but he thinks the sannyasin is the renunciate. In fact, worldly men look at sannyasins with pity: ”Poor things, they have left everything. They have missed all of life’s pleasures.” They revere sannyasins and deep down pity them, too: ”Poor things! They have renounced everything without enjoying anything. At least some things they should have enjoyed!” But worldly man have no ideas whom they are talking about. The sannyasin has experienced the greatest enjoyment. He has been invited by the vast existence to partake of the greatest of all enjoyments. One doesn't need to give up anything.Only what is needed is to know, to taste. This very taste then will slowly displace all that is useless and trivial in life. The useless just falls away; it needs not to be renounced.
