Jai Gurudev!
As Ram Navami is approaching, it is good to remind ourselves what does it stand for? Lord Rama came when it was necessary to establish dharma, the righteous way. It is the same with all great avatars. They come in times of great upheavals, the great changes in the world.
Rama killed many demons. Eventually, he killed the main demon: Ravana. Ravana, as Guruji explained, represents our pride. So all these demons stand for some negative quality we have to remove in order to make place for the positive quality.
If we understand the life and significance behind Rama's incarnation, then we can draw the right lessons which can help us to improve our lives; to be better husband, wife, colleague, boss, friend, etc and above anything else: better disciple. No matter where God has placed us, we can fulfill all our duties in the best possible way. Thus, we will reap the best possible results, leading us to everlasting happiness and fulfillment of life.
We have to learn how to surrender in love and humility because only through surrendering our pride and ego we can grow as humans, so that we can become devotees leading us up to be true disciples. The word 'disciple' means to accept the discipline of someone who can help us correct our ways. The problem with most people is that we think that we know the best therefore we don't need anyone to tell us what is right for us. As long as we stay in that mind frame we are living in hell. The moment we accept the fact that we know nothing is when our spiritual journey can begin.
So let us all open up to the discipline and guidance of our Satguru, Sri Swami Vishwananda who is, without doubt, qualified to lead us from the mire of ignorance and wrong ways to the highest potential that our souls can ever crave for: unity with the Divine. Jai Gurudev!
Swami Vishwakanjalochanananda, Croatia

Spanish Darshan Tour 2016 (12 May 2016 – 17 May 2016)
We are excited to announce a new pilgrimage taking place in Spain. The pilgrimage itself is called, “Following the Trail of the Virgin Mary.” A few of the Holy pilgrimage sites include, the Santa Maria de Montserrat monastery, the Cave of Saint Ignatius of Manresa, and the Basilica of Our Lady of the Abandoned (“Virgen de los Desamparados”). Also included in the pilgrimage will be two Darshans with Sri Swami Vishwananda.
For more information, please visit: www.bhaktimarga.org/events/event/spanish-darshan-tour-2016-following-trail-virgin-mary.

Sacred Places of Tamil Nadu – South India Pilgrimage with Mahamandaleshwar Sri Swami Vishwananda (5 December 2016- 18 December 2016)
Directly after the Inauguration of the Bhakti Marga Ashram in Vrindavan, there is the opportunity to go on a pilgrimage with Guruji to Tamil Nadu, South India. Tamil Nadu has been the home of countless Acharyas, Saints and Avatars. It is also one of the main places of Shiva worship and various Maha Shakti Peethas. We will visit the mountain Arunachala, Maha Shakti Peethas, Sri Rangam – the main temple of all Vaishnavas and especially of the followers of Sri Sampradaya - and many more beautiful places and Saints.
Discover more at: www.bhaktimarga.org/southindia2016.

The Essence of Shreemad Bhagavatam
AVAILABLE IN BHAKTI SHOP AT SHREE PEETHA NILAYA
The Shreemad Bhagavatam is the great epic that glorifies Bhakti Yoga, the yoga of devotion. In this scripture, Sri Swami Vishwananda gives commentary on the fundamental principles and teachings so that one may begin to have a comprehensive understanding of the Shreemad Bhagavatam.
Sri Gopi Gita
AVAILABLE IN BHAKTI SHOP AT SHREE PEETHA NILAYA
The Gopi Gita is the “Song of Separation” sung by the Gopis as they were longing for their beloved Krishna. Sri Swami Vishwananda has given an inspiring and insightful commentary about the supreme devotion of the Gopis, which illustrates the beauty of their relationship they had with Lord Krishna.
