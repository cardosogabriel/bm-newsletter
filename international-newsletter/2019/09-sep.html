SEPTEMBER 2019, #53
Our Connection with Gurudev and Krishna
Swami Vishwaanashuyananda, SPN
Jai Gurudev Everyone,
It’s been a beautiful time as Vaishnavas to celebrate one of our most important events of the year: Krishna Janmashtami, the birth of the Lord of all universes manifested on our beloved Mother Earth. For me it’s a great reminder of the sacrifice that takes place where the Lord of all brings himself down to earth, to our level, to be here for us! Essentially this is what Guruji has done. He has given us this opportunity to develop this loving relationship with him and with all the deities – Radha Krishna, Narasimha, Lakshmi, etc.
It’s a great grace that few people in this life have the possibility to be connected to a manifestation of the Lord himself (the Satguru) …and it’s also through the deities that we are given the opportunity of seva: to render service to the Beloved in the form of the Master/Deity. Through the service and through Guruji’s Grace we can establish an inner relationship with them. Guruji has said so many times that ‘God is waiting inside of your hearts, banging on the door to be let out, but we don’t listen and don’t open the door.’ So at every turn and every opportunity, He gives us the chance to turn and focus on Him, and for Him to be the centre of our attention. Very much like the lifetime of Krishna when He would act mischievously and colour Radha and the gopis, Guruji gives us the same opportunity during His Holi pilgrimages in India (and during the Just Love Festival Holi too)! The same way that the gopis and gopas would have this connection to Krishna, we have the same connection with Guruji.
The same way that Yashoda would clean and cook for her darling Krishna, the same way many of you have cleaned and cooked for Guruji when He traveled around the world. He is giving us the same opportunity of relationship that Krishna gave 5000 years ago. When He is not physically present, He is giving us this opportunity through the deities.
The same way that Krishna danced with the gopis in the forests of Vrindavan, Guruji was dancing on the day of Krishna Janmashtami with the Dandiya sticks with devotees outside His bungalow. With any opportunity, He is looking to develop this relationship with you. When offering your puja, when you connect with the deities, let the doors of your heart open and let the divine within your heart be revealed to you through Guruji’s Grace. Whether it’s through Kirtan, Japa, Puja or any path of Bhakti, know that Guruji is here to reveal this relationship of love within you…no matter how long it takes! Connect, remember and cherish your relationship with Guruji and your Ishtadev! If Guruji is an incarnation of Narayana, and all deities come from Narayana, then when you are loving your Ishtadev, are you not loving Gurudev?
Connect, serve and remember that in any way, any where, our Gurudev is inviting us to develop our relationship with him.
Happy Krishna Janmashtami!
Swami Vishwaanashuyananda

This year, devotees from Czech, Poland, Slovakia, Slovenia, Albania, Turkey, Croatia, and Romania are invited to join the most amazing and joyful festival of the year in Vrindavan with Paramahamsa Vishwananda.

Message of the Month
Jai Gurudev,
Why is the tilak for Panduranga different? Because He is Krishna and Panduranga is none other than Krishna, no? In the North, we talk about different aspects of Lord Krishna and the Panduranga Leela is the fourth aspect of Krishna. When Krishna did the Leela of Panduranga, He was not only Himself; in that aspect of Panduranga, Vitthala, there was also Shiva with Him.
There is a Shivalingam on Panduranga’s head. This is because when Krishna was going to Rukmini to enact the Leela as Panduranga, He first went to Shiva and said, ‘I am going to incarnate to do that Leela now, so you come with Me also.’ And Shiva came on the top of Panduranga.
