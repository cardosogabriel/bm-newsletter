NOVEMBER 2019, #55
Grace
Swami Vishwarevatikaantananda, SPN
Jai Gurudev, dear Bhakti Marga family,
On this most-auspicious month of Kartik, it seems most appropriate to me to write about Grace. During this month it is said that every spiritual act we do reaps far greater rewards than usual. And as special and exciting as that is, that is exactly what Grace is, and in truth, that Grace is available at all times and to every striving soul. The best way I can explain what Grace is, is to compare it to justice. Justice is when I do something and get the perfectly proportionate reward for it. Grace is when I do something and get a wildly disproportionate reward for it.
But the one thing I feel like people often fail to notice in that equation is the first part: we do ‘something’. We tend to focus on the ‘disproportionate reward’ part but we forget that it doesn’t come for free. Vaishnavism places a lot of emphasis on surrender, on placing all our trust in God’s hands, but many times I’ve seen how that is a double-edged sword. On the one hand, it’s the only way to truly combat our ego and to cut out our arrogance. But on the other hand, I see how it also creates an irresponsible attitude of complacency and escapism. Because God is so great, because Guruji is so ready to help us, we should place our full trust and depend on Him. But also, because God is so great, because Guruji is so ready to help us, we tend to become lazy and expect that they will take care of everything.
This entire situation comes from the frequent powerlessness that we encounter in our day-to-day lives. In so many of life’s biggest challenges, we feel utterly helpless, unable to change the circumstances around our lives. In that helplessness it is absolutely essential that we turn to God’s Grace. But it is so vital that we do so with the understanding that, whilst we may not be sufficiently powerful to do whatever it is that we desire, it does not mean that we have NO power to change anything.
In short, what I am trying to say is that we must always try to do the maximum we can, with the tools we’ve already been given by God and Guru before we even begin to think of receiving new tools and support. Vaishnavism is all about a relationship, the greatest and most important one of all, and as in any healthy relationship, it’s all about meeting somewhere in the middle.
Kartik is the time when God simply reduces how much you have to do, for Him to step in and do the rest. If you really understand Vaishnavism, if you really understand Grace, and if you really understand the incredibly unique opportunity it is to take initiation from your Satguru, what more would you need to feel excited and motivated to go out there and do something!
Swami Revatikaanta

SERVE A DEITY
We want to offer you an exclusive opportunity to serve the main deities at Shree Peetha Nilaya, the home of the Divine Mother.
What is the Serve A Deity Program?
This new opportunity enables individuals to actively assist the pujaris who serve the deities every day. Many people would like to assist in service towards the Divine but do not know how...now we are making it possible for you to help by sponsoring the deities.  
The donations provided through 'Serve A Deity' covers the costs for prasad, puja items, flowers, electricity, water, etc. Every time a puja is conducted in the temple, the blessing of this goes to every sponsor, no matter where they are, or from which country they come. In this way, you are helping the deity to be served and, at the same time, the blessing from the puja directly serves those who are supporting.

Message of the Month
Jai Gurudev, everybody!
Today we celebrate the second day of Maha Devi by the name of Brahmacharini.
Actually, Brahmacharini, is not only for the brahmacharis. You know, very often people think that Brahmacharini, it is only for those who have renounced and live a certain way. Of course, when we look at Brahmacharini, She holds a rosary and She holds a pot. That mala which She holds symbolises knowledge. Knowledge is infinite. Like, you think that you know something, but there is always something to learn. Knowledge never finishes. And the mala also represents bhakti, because if knowledge doesn’t awaken bhakti inside of you, that knowledge is useless. When we talk about knowledge on the outside, we talk about education.
