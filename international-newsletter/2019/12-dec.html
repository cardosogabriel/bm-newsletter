Swamini Pratibha Maa, SPN
Importance of Change
In tonight's satsang, Guruji spoke about how hard it is for people to change; how our addictions and patterns of behaviour, dictate our life, and keep us from succeeding.
He appeared to King Satyavrata in a time when the King had lost all hope. His people had stopped caring about higher truths, and were mired in the trivial pursuits of the world. The mind of man was corrupt.
‘For the protection of the good, for the destruction of the wicked, and for the establishment of righteousness, I incarnate from age to age.’ Bhagavad Gita 4:8
The King was pondering the disastrous state of the world while he stood in the river and scooped up a handful of water. To his surprise, a tiny fish was there, talking to him! It begged him for protection from the sea creatures ready to eat him. The King decided this was the perfect distraction from the weight of the world he was carrying, and put the fish in his water pot.
He took it home, and went about his duties. When he checked on the little fish later, it had outgrown the pot! And it kept growing, forcing the king to take it to a nearby lake. After a fitful night’s sleep, he rushed to the lake to see a massive fish stretching from bank to bank.
As the fish kept changing, the King kept adjusting, until finally he left his duties to pull the fish out to sea. Strangely, all of this brought him great joy. The love of life, that had been sucked out of him by the material world, was returning. As he travelled with the fish, it became more and more effulgent and other-worldly. By the time they reached the sea, the King knew this fish was Lord Narayana Himself.
As a result of his selfless service to the fish, and his ability to keep changing with the circumstances, he was taken into the next age along with the Saptarishis when the world was brought to an end.
These are the times we are in now. Focusing on the material world is leading many to destruction.
It is time to change.
And the Guru is in front of us now, asking us to trust Him. I'm sure King Satyavrata's friends and family thought he'd lost his mind when he left the kingdom, pulling a fish in a cart out to sea. How many of our concerned friends wish we would drop spiritual seeking and stay with the status quo, even if it means going over Niagara Falls in a barrel?
Your heart is calling. It wants this life to be the ultimate. Seek with intensity! Jump out of that societal box! Allow the love of the Guru to show you the best way to live and change.
Jai Gurudev!
Swamini Pratibha Maa

HOLI 2020
This year, devotees from Czech, Poland, Slovakia, Slovenia, Albania, Turkey, Croatia, N. Macedonia, Romania and Greece are invited to join the most amazing and joyful festival of the year in Vrindavan with Paramahamsa Vishwananda.
Holi is known as the festival of colours and the festival of sharing love. It is the most joyful time in India. People light bonfires symbolising the victory of good over evil, forget about their concerns, and throw colours and water on each other in celebration of the fresh starts of spring.

Message of the Month
Jai Gurudev,
Question: What does it take to be a sincere devotee?
Oh, that’s difficult!
You said it in the question itself: sincerity. You have to know what a devotee is. If you have taken initiation as a devotee, you have given yourself to the Lord, no? That’s what it is to be a devotee: you are here to serve Him and you have placed yourself in His care. Being a devotee doesn’t mean only to wear the tilak and the kanti (Tulsi necklace) and go around with your mala bag, and say, ‘I am a devotee.’ No, it’s not that. A devotee has love.
When we take initiation as devotees, a Vaishnava tradition is that we have to have the same quality as the Supreme Lord Himself. We can’t be different from Him. He is the Master; He is the Lord; He is the Beloved; we have to reflect Him as a devotees If you don’t reflect Him, then how can you call yourself a devotee? So, first that quality must awaken inside of you.
