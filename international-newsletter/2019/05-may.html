Spring Navaratri at The Ashram
Swamini Vishwa Godaavari Mata, France
Spring is a time of rebirth and new growth; it’s also a time to take care of our body, mind and soul.
The Ashram underwent a renovation recently, which gave it a feeling of new space; our bodies had a detox with the healthy food of The Ashram kitchen; and the Alpha & Omega bed recharged our batteries. This shows how the Ashram teams are relentlessly working to support our spiritual blossoming by offering the best conditions of stay during spiritual events. Travelling to different places in India and staying in other ashrams, it is clear that Shree Peetha Nilaya is the most beautiful and unique place. However, having the best conditions outside are not enough for our inner growth. As Paramahamsa Vishwananda often says, the biggest transformation has to take place inside of us.
Every year, the spring festivities come to remind us about the inner purification through which we have to go: the symbolic victory of light over darkness inside us and the surrendering of the mind to the power of the heart and devotion. Once we conquer the inner demons of our minds, Peace and Love triumph in our inner space. This phenomena is mirrored in the main Hindu spiritual festivals during the first months of the year.
First of all, Holi celebration comes as an occasion to gather, perform religious rituals in front of the fire and pray for the internal evil to be destroyed, much like the way that Holika, the sister of the demon Hiranyakashipu, was killed in the fire. The second big festival celebrating the victory of light is Navaratri, when Goddess Durga battled and emerged victorious over the buffalo demon Mahishasura to help restore Dharma. The demon represents our ego which has to be subdued for our soul to join and serve the Divine. The lights and the rituals during several days are there to awaken the inner light of Love and to come out from the mind's grip.
This year, we celebrated the Spring Navaratri for the first time in our ashram. It was a new opportunity in our calendar to remind ourselves of the necessity to accept inner transformation. Guruji clarified that this process includes "tests to pass". The same way that metal has to melt to be restructured, our system will go through a "test period". Patience and regular Sadhana practice are precious friends until the confusion of the mind is finally dispelled.
Spring is also called Chaitra, which means "beginning of creation". Each Spring, we have this opportunity of death, resurrection, and re-creation. May we all resurrect from the sleep of ignorance and triumph over the mind's negative influence.
If, little by little, you get rid of the negativity of the mind, and if the mind is transcended and becomes pure, everything you do will be pure. There will be no negative present inside of you. The power of the mind is so great that you will create what you want. If you train your mind to be positive at all times, you will create positive things.
—Paramahamsa Sri Swami Vishwananda

Message of the Month
Jai Gurudev,
May the blessing of Panduranga and Rukmini be to all of you, and especially those who have helped.
You see, building a temple is not the aim of everybody. Not everybody has this Grace of participating in building a temple. A temple is a place where the Lord resides. Today we are celebrating Ram Navami, no? Rama Himself is a reminder of the inner temple. ‘Ra’ means temple, ‘ma’ means inside. So, Rama means something which is inside. So, on this auspicious day, we also make this beautiful temple of Panduranga and Rukmini a reminder of what you have inside of you.
